> A **schema** is the **logical structure or blueprint of a database**.

It defines:
- Tables
- Columns
- Data types
- Relationships
- Constraints

In simple words:

> **Schema = design of the database**

It describes **how data is organized**, not the actual data.

---
## Example Schema
Suppose we design a **Student Database**.

**Students Table** :

|Column|Type|
|---|---|
|student_id|INT|
|name|VARCHAR|
|age|INT|

**Courses Table** :

|Column|Type|
|---|---|
|course_id|INT|
|course_name|VARCHAR|

This structure is the **schema**.
