**Design Principles** are high-level guidelines or best practices used to design clean, maintainable, and scalable software. They focus on _how code should be structured_ and _how responsibilities should be divided_. Examples include **SOLID**, **DRY**, **KISS**, and **Separation of Concerns**.

**Design Patterns** are reusable, proven solutions to common software design problems. They provide _standard ways to implement code_ for specific scenarios. Examples include **Singleton**, **Factory**, **Observer**, and **Strategy**.
