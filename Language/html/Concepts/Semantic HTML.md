**Semantic tags** are HTML elements that **clearly describe their meaning or purpose** both for the browser and for humans.

> They give **context** to the content, unlike generic `<div>` or `<span>` which have no meaning.

Examples :

|Tag|Purpose / Use|
|---|---|
|`<header>`|Page or section header (logo, nav, title)|
|`<nav>`|Navigation links|
|`<main>`|Main content of the page|
|`<section>`|A thematic section of content|
|`<article>`|Independent, self-contained content (blog post, news article)|
|`<aside>`|Sidebar, related content, or ads|
|`<footer>`|Footer of page or section (copyright, contact)|
|`<figure>`|Media content with optional `<figcaption>`|
|`<figcaption>`|Caption/description for `<figure>`|
|`<mark>`|Highlighted text|
|`<time>`|Date/time info|
|`<address>`|Contact info|
